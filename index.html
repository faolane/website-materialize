About me

My PhD work focused on the modelling of nuclear quantum effects in simulations and the study of their impact on the diffusion of protons in perovskite materials. Research experience

2013 - 2016

PhD thesis

Modelling and simulation of quantum effects in molecular dynamics: application to the study of proton conduction

This thesis deals with the study and development of numerical methods to include nuclear quantum effects in Molecular Dynamics (MD) simulations. In particular, we study the Quantum Thermal Bath (QTB) method proposed by Dammak and coworkers. We also present a combination of the QTB method with Path Integral MD simulations (QTB-PIMD) that allows to reduce the computational cost of standard PIMD simulations. We then use these methods to study the impact of quantum effects on the proton diffusion in perovskite materials.   More info >>

2013

2nd year of Master Internship

Sudy of proton transfer in ice using the Quantum Thermal Bath method

During the first part of this internship, we have studied the Quantum Thermal Bath (QTB) method on simple one-dimensional systems and implemented the method on the QuantumEspresso package for electronic-structure calculations (DFT). In a second part, we have investigated the impact of quantum effects on collective proton transfer in ice Ih using the QTB method and first-principles MD simulations.

2012

1st year of Master Internship

Frustrated magnetism of classical Heisenberg spins on a kagome lattice

During this internsip, we have studied the behavior of a classical frustrated Heisenberg model on a Kagome lattice using replica exchange Monte Carlo. Frustration in this sytem is responsible for existence of different possible ground-states for the system depending on the values for the exchange interaction between the spins. Here we focused on the cuboc2 phase (see this reference for more infos on the phase diagram of this system). In particular, we have investigated how the spin disorder affects the powder-averaged structure factor. Some of the results obtained are available as supplemental material of this paper.
